Prologue: Untitled Chip-8 Emulator

Throughout my life, I have always been interested in computers. Whether that be fixing them, programming them, building them, or anything else, I have always been interested in them. One thing that had always stuck out to me was emulators. I was amazed at how my old GBA games were able to be played perfectly on a computer. Around 2015, I had decided that I wanted to make one of my own.

I began to search around to see what I wanted to make an emulator of. I had the knowledge back then to know that starting with something complex, like the GBA, would be basically impossible at my skill level. I eventually found some resources saying that Chip-8 is the easiest to make. I found a Chip-8 Emulator tutorial online and got to work... but also not. I, uhh... didn't know how to program.

Being the masochistic person I was, instead of picking something easy to learn like Python, I dove head first into C++, which is one of the most difficult Programming Languages to learn. Then, I got to work.

I remember following the tutorial as much as I possibly could, as I didn't want to mess up anything. After about a day of coding, it was "done". I ran it and.... it didn't work. Being the beginner to programming that I was, I didn't know what was wrong or how to fix it. You would think that I would've given up here, but nah. Instead of giving up, I for some unfathomable reason decided that, not only did I want to make a Gameboy emulator, but I wanted to make it without following any tutorials, and without copying any code. I told myself that it would be easy. (It was not.)
